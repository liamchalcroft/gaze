## ABSTRACT (Submission Text)

**Purpose**
Rare neurological conditions affect millions globally yet remain poorly served by AI systems trained predominantly on common pathologies. We developed an agentic framework enabling vision-language models to autonomously conduct multi-turn analysis with tool use, creating transparent reasoning traces while acquiring specialist knowledge in real-time without model fine-tuning.

**Methods and Materials**
We evaluated on NOVA, a challenging brain MRI benchmark (900+ scans, 281 rare conditions). Six configurations were compared: baseline single-turn, reasoning-enhanced, agentic multi-turn, and comprehensive agents combining extended reasoning, visual tools (zoom, crop, contrast, threshold), and PubMed literature retrieval. Models autonomously controlled analysis continuation and tool selection, generating interpretable decision traces.

**Results**

Agentic configurations demonstrated improved localisation (mAP@50) and caption quality (METEOR) versus baseline. The comprehensive agent showed substantial gains, particularly for rare conditions, while producing auditable reasoning chains with cited evidence sources.

**Conclusion**
Agentic reasoning enables practical deployment of AI for rare pathology analysis without costly retraining. The interpretable multi-turn approach with evidence retrieval addresses key barriers to clinical AI adoption: specialist knowledge gaps, hallucination risk, and lack of transparency.
